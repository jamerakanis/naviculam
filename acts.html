Activities

In our park for have lodges for overnight campers and guided night tours with camping for enthusiasts wanting to enjoy an untouched Canadian night sky. Some things you can enjoy are treetop trekking, with a 400 metre zip line over one of our many lakes. You can sail in the summer months and enjoy both summer fishing and winter ice fishing with our trained staff keeping a watchful eye over you. If you want some sport, worry not. We have diving in the summer and cross country skiing in the winter months with our top of the line Skevik skis. If hiking is not to your liking you can rent one of our many bikes. All bikes are provided by our neighbour right here from Kona (the Stinky and Operator). For people wanting to learn about the vast outdoors we have our trained staff giving lessons for everything from sailing to biking and skiing. You can go trekking at Hawke island and enjoy a large variety of wild birds and experience the raw, preplexing beauty.

Wildlife Watching

Our park is located on the southern coast and offers awe-inspiring views of the horizon. We give you the chance to become one with the ocean while viewing some of the largest whales in the world including humpbacks and getting a chance to view some of the 35 million sea birds that call Newfoundland and Labrador home. Our coastline is home to some of the most tranquil views in the world with sightings of the 10,000 year glacial giants coming into sight on more than a few occasions. You can enjoy are wide variety of berries from black and blueberries to crow and cloudberries. You can also check out our provincial plant, the one and only pitcher plant! He's carnivorous, but worry not; we have nets around the plants. You can also view the vast range wildflowers in the form of rare orchids and plants.
